Impacts Of Mining.

Why do We Mine?

People mine for many reasons, but the most important reason is to extract necessary materials for construction. In our society, almost everything is created from the materials that we gathered from the Earth. Buildings, roads, machinery, and other things that benefits our life are supported by mining resources from this planet.

Without mining, we would be running out of resources all the time to create these essential products in society. However, is this really worth the damage we've caused to the environment, and is there a better way to mine instead?

Extracting Metals.

Mining for Materials

Mining materials is about getting raw materials from the environment that'll later be modified in different ways based on what it's going to be used for.


This is the part that hurts the environment the most. Even though the extracted resources can later be used to benefit our lifes, mining can also be very harmful in nature. Some of the most popular ways of mining is by digging the large area of surface, digging underground, and sifting minerals out of water.

Extractive Metallurgy

Extractive Metallurgy is the part where raw ores are turned into purer metals that people can use in constructions.


People who work in this field break down large ores into either valuable metals or wastes. The impurities of an metal can be removed through chemistry in 3 different ways. The first method is through pyrometallurgy, where the material can be either roasted or smelted using heat. The difference between these two is that smelting melts the metal into it's pure liquid form while roasting does not melt the metal. For example, roasting sulfide ores allows sulfur to escape as sulfur dioxide. Another method is through hydrometallurgy, where the materials are dissolved in acidic or basic solutions to extract the metals. After this leaching step, one of the compound will be precipitated for the metal to become pure.
Another part of Extractice Metallurgy is creating steel from metals. This can be done by blowing oxygen into carbon rich irons to create low carbon steels, or by using electricity to charge up the furnace to melt ores then mixed with carbon to create steel.

Nuclear Engineering

Nuclear engineering is the process where radioactive materials are mined and processed to be used as a source of fuel,munitions, and as medicine. This corresponds greatly whit metallurgical engineering.


People from all around the world mine radioactive isotopes from deep beneath the Earth.In order to determine if the metal is worthy of being mined Geological Composition is used. Geological Composition refers to both the types of minerals within a rock and the overall chemical makeup of the rock. Another very effective way to determine this is to use X-rays and or drilling holes to determine if there is any radioactive rock in that part of land. Uranium the most common radioactive material is a byproduct of copper mining. Companies organise these metals based on a grading scale where low grade is about .02% U and high grade is 20% U. However due to the dangers of radiation a new technique was developed to mine it which is called dust suppression. However poorer countries allow the radioactive metal to be mined like any other element. Normally these elements would be mined by digging up large portions of land to find the meattl. But something specific for mining Uranium is dissolving it in water then pumping it up. This is ideal since it causes the least disruption of the land. However it is banned if there is a source of drinkable water nearby.

Environmental Impacts.

Pollution Mining can cause both water and air pollution in the environment. Toxic wastes and using machines for mining can cause serious air pollutions near the area. Chemicals can also be leaked into nature, causing water pollution.
Loss of Biodiversity Air pollution can cause damage to land animals because the dangerous air that they breathe can harm their organs, resulting in possible deaths. Water pollution cause damage by depleting oxygen in the water, suffocating aquatic animals while also poisoning them with leaked chemicals. These problems lead to a weak ecosystem, creating even more problem in nature.
Soil Erosion The process of leaching in hydrometallurgy and mining cause soil erosions. Soil erosion can be a big problem because it decrease water quality, prevent agriculture, sinkholes, mudslides, and other prolems in the terrain.

Call to Action.

These problems are still occuring right now, but there's something we all can do to help!

  • Protest!

    • More restricted mining sites
    • Improving the methods used in extracting materials.
  • Recycle materials instead of mining new material.
  • Investing and donating for healthier mining technology
